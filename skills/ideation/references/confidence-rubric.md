# Evidence Gate Rubric

Use this rubric to judge readiness before generating a contract.

## Rules

- Readiness is not a number.
- Each gate is either `ready` or `not-ready`.
- When unsure, choose `not-ready`.
- Proceed to contract only when all 5 gates are ready, or the user ends the interview early.

## Gates

### Problem Clarity

Do I understand what problem we're solving, who has it, and why it matters?

Ready when the problem is concrete: who, what, when, and impact.

### Goal Definition

Are the goals specific and measurable?

Ready when each goal states a measurable outcome.

### Success Criteria

Can every goal be checked pass/fail today?

Ready when each criterion has a test, command, or explicit human judgment call.

### Scope Boundaries

Do I know what's in and out of scope?

Ready when in-scope, out-of-scope, and future considerations are explicit.

### Consistency

Are there contradictions I need resolved?

Ready when requirements align and priorities are clear.

## Question Best Practices

- Be specific.
- Offer options.
- Reference context.
- Prioritize the weakest gate.
- Ask chained questions one at a time.

## Spec Feedback Quality

Use this during spec review, not during contract formation.

- Strong: feedback strategy exists and all iterative components have loops.
- Adequate: some loops are missing or vague.
- Weak: no feedback strategy or major gaps in loops.
